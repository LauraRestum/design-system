# Audience Personas

Every audience Envision serves is a key player in the mission. These personas are **audience lenses** — types of people who interact with Envision, mapped to the pillars they're most likely to touch. The same person may interact with multiple pillars at different points in their relationship with Envision.

Sourced from the Envision Brand Book (2026 Edition), Section 5.

> Every message is an opportunity to help someone thrive. Make it count.

---

## Vision Rehabilitation Client

**Who they are:** An adult experiencing vision loss — newly diagnosed or facing progressive deterioration — actively seeking clinical support and rehabilitation services. Often referred by an ophthalmologist or optometrist. Frequently the first point of contact with Envision, arriving at a moment of emotional vulnerability and uncertainty.

**What they need:** Clinical credibility, practical tools, and hope grounded in real outcomes. They need to know that losing vision is not losing a life.

**Pillars:** Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center primarily. Everyday Store, assistive technology training, and Mission Services as their journey continues.

**How to reach them:** Physician referral is the primary entry point. Warm, credible, outcome-focused materials. Never pity, never charity framing.

**What Thriving looks like:** Regaining daily function and confidence. Moving from uncertainty to independence with the right tools and support.

## BVI Adult

**Who they are:** An adult who is blind or low vision who has been navigating life with vision loss for some time. May have been born with a visual impairment or lost their sight earlier in life. Not in active clinical rehabilitation — living, working, and engaging with community on their own terms.

**What they need:** Community, connection, and programs that meet them where they are. They are not looking to be helped — they are looking to participate.

**Pillars:** Mission Services primarily. Arts Center, Workforce Innovation, and Continuing Education depending on interests and life stage.

**How to reach them:** Peer-to-peer channels, community networks, word of mouth. Tone is direct and respectful. **Never patronizing.**

**What Thriving looks like:** A full life on their own terms. Community, purpose, and the freedom to engage with Envision as a participant, not a recipient.

---

## BVI Youth

**Who they are:** A young person who is blind or low vision, ages 14-22, navigating the transition from school to adulthood. May be the only student with vision loss in their entire school district. Figuring out who they are, what they want, and what's possible — often without peers who share their experience.

**What they need:** Connection with peers who get it. Real skills, real career exploration, and a clear picture of what their future can look like. **Not inspiration — possibility.**

**Pillars:** Workforce Innovation and Level-Up primarily. Mission Services through Heather's Camp as a younger entry point.

**How to reach them:** The primary marketing audience for youth programs is **TVIs, VR counselors, parents, and educators** — not the youth directly. Materials targeting youth should feel peer-level, energetic, and possibility-focused. **Never condescending.**

**What Thriving looks like:** A young person who arrives at adulthood with direction, skills, confidence, and a network of peers who understand their experience.

## Family Member or Caregiver

**Who they are:** A parent, spouse, adult child, or close family member navigating vision loss alongside someone they love. Often doing the research, making the calls, and managing the logistics while also managing their own fear and uncertainty.

**What they need:** Clarity, reassurance, and practical guidance. They need to understand what's available, what to expect, and how to support their person without taking over. **They are not the client — but they are often the decision-maker.**

**Pillars:** Vision Rehabilitation Center, ECDC, Mission Services, and Workforce Innovation depending on who they are supporting and at what life stage.

**How to reach them:** Physician referral, school and TVI networks, community word of mouth. Tone is warm, practical, and empowering. **Never alarmist.**

**What Thriving looks like:** Confidence that their person is supported, equipped, and not alone. The knowledge that they don't have to figure this out by themselves either.

## Referring Professional

**Who they are:** A credentialed professional whose role connects people with vision loss to Envision's programs. Includes TVIs, vocational rehabilitation counselors, ophthalmologists, optometrists, occupational therapists, social workers.

**What they need:** Confidence that Envision will deliver. Clinical credibility, clear program documentation, outcome data, and materials that make the referral process easy.

**Pillars:** Vision Rehabilitation Center, Workforce Innovation, ECDC, and Mission Services depending on professional focus.

**How to reach them:** Professional networks, state AER chapters, TVI listservs, continuing education events, peer referral. Tone is collegial and outcomes-focused. **Write to their credential level.**

**What Thriving looks like:** A client who gets the right support at the right time. A referral process that is straightforward and well-documented.

---

## Continuing Education Professional

**Who they are:** A credentialed professional in vision rehabilitation or blindness services seeking ongoing professional development. Includes TVIs, orientation and mobility specialists, occupational therapists, low vision clinicians, researchers. They engage with Envision as a peer and thought leader.

**What they need:** High-quality, relevant continuing education that advances their practice. CEU credits, access to emerging research, connection with peers across the field.

**Pillars:** Continuing Education primarily. Research Institute as interests intersect with applied vision science.

**How to reach them:** Professional associations, conference networks, peer referral, direct outreach through TVI and rehabilitation listservs. Tone is authoritative and collegial.

**What Thriving looks like:** A field that is more connected, better informed, and more equipped to serve people who are blind or low vision.

## Enterprise Buyer

**Who they are:** A procurement officer, supply chain manager, operations leader, or contracting officer evaluating Envision as a commercial partner for print, fulfillment, contact center, assembly, or products. **Not motivated by mission — motivated by performance, compliance, and reliability.**

**What they need:** Proof that Envision can deliver. On-time, at spec, at scale. Certifications, compliance documentation, capability demonstrations, and references.

**Pillars:** Commercial Solutions primarily across both commercial and government tracks.

**How to reach them:** Direct sales outreach, industry events, vendor networks, RFP processes. Tone is professional, capability-led, credibility-forward. **Never lead with nonprofit framing.**

**What Thriving looks like:** A vendor relationship that performs consistently, reduces friction, and delivers on every commitment.

---

## Military Community

**Who they are:** Active duty service members, veterans, military spouses, and military families who shop at Envision Base Supply Centers. Also includes installation leadership and contracting officers who oversee BSC operations and AbilityOne compliance.

**What they need:** Reliable products, a well-run store, and a supply chain that doesn't let them down. Contracting officers additionally need compliance documentation and AbilityOne credentials.

**Pillars:** Envision Base Supply Center primarily. Commercial Solutions for contracting officers.

**How to reach them:** On-installation presence, installation leadership relationships, military community networks. Tone is direct, respectful, no-nonsense. **Shared values over sentimentality.**

**What Thriving looks like:** A Base Supply Center that runs smoothly, stocks what they need, and operates with the professionalism the military community expects.

---

## Arts Community

**Who they are:** Artists who are blind or low vision, arts patrons, educators, cultural partners, and community members who engage with the Envision Arts Center. Some are creators. Some are supporters. All are drawn by the belief that creative expression belongs to everyone.

**What they need:** A space that takes the work seriously. Programming that is high quality, welcoming, and expansive. **Not a charity art show — a genuine creative community with real artists making real work.**

**Pillars:** Envision Arts Center primarily. Mission Services and Donor/Community Partner depending on engagement level.

**How to reach them:** Local arts networks, community events, social media, word of mouth. Tone is expressive, celebratory, community-rooted. **Never clinical or institutional.**

**What Thriving looks like:** An artist whose work is seen, valued, and celebrated. A community that experiences creative possibility without limitation.

---

## Research and Academic Community

**Who they are:** Researchers, university faculty, fellows, industry partners, and clinicians who engage with Envision Research Institute. Motivated by discovery, scientific rigor, and the application of research to real-world outcomes. They engage with Envision as a peer institution.

**What they need:** A credible, well-resourced research environment. Access to facilities, data, fellowship opportunities, and collaborative partnerships that advance the field.

**Pillars:** Envision Research Institute primarily. Continuing Education and Vision Rehabilitation Center as work intersects with clinical application.

**How to reach them:** Academic conferences, peer-reviewed publications, university partnerships, professional networks. Tone is precise, credible, collegial.

**What Thriving looks like:** A breakthrough that changes how vision loss is understood or treated. A fellowship that launches a career. A partnership that produces something that didn't exist before.

---

## Donor or Community Partner

**Who they are:** Individual givers, major donors, foundations, corporate partners, and community organizations who invest in Envision's mission financially or through partnership. Motivated by impact — they want to know their investment is making a real difference in real lives.

**What they need:** Proof of impact. Inspiring stories of independence and transformation. Quantified outcomes where possible. A relationship that feels personal and reciprocal.

**Pillars:** Foundations primarily for philanthropic giving. Any pillar depending on area of interest.

**How to reach them:** Donor events, foundation relationships, personal outreach, annual reports, impact communications. Tone is grateful, impact-driven, relationship-focused. **Lead with stories, back them with outcomes.**

**What Thriving looks like:** Knowing their investment created something real. A relationship with Envision that deepens over time.

---

## Envision Employee or Job Seeker

**Who they are:** Someone who works at Envision or is actively seeking employment there. Includes people who are blind or low vision working in manufacturing, contact center, or other roles, as well as sighted employees across all departments. Also includes BVI individuals pursuing employment through Envision's mission and general candidates applying for open positions.

**What they need:** A workplace that values their contribution, invests in their growth, and lives its mission from the inside out.

**Pillars:** All pillars depending on role. Commercial Solutions and Base Supply Centers for manufacturing and retail employees. Mission Services and Workforce Innovation for BVI employees hired through the mission pipeline.

**How to reach them:** Job listings, employee referral, workforce development partnerships, internal communications. Tone is motivating, mission-connected, respectful.

**What Thriving looks like:** A career that grows. A workplace that sees them fully. The knowledge that their work, whatever their role, contributes to something that matters.
