# Pillar Profiles

Envision operates across eight program and service areas. Each has its own audience, tone, and messaging priorities, but all connect back to the master brand and the central idea of Thriving.

Sourced from the Envision Brand Book (2026 Edition), Section 4.

---

## The Five Mission Pillars

Everything Envision does connects back to five foundational pillars drawn from our mission. Every program and service area maps to one or more of these.

- **Employment** — Career pathways, job readiness, assistive technology training, competitive employment.
- **Outreach** — Community-based programs, peer support, partnerships that extend Envision's reach.
- **Rehabilitation** — Clinical and adaptive skills training — orientation and mobility, daily living.
- **Education** — Early childhood development, integrated classrooms, continuing education for professionals.
- **Research** — Clinical trials, technology development, published research in vision science.

## Envision Arts Center

**What it does:** A studio, gallery, and community space dedicated to creative expression by and for people who are blind or low vision. Currently centered on traditional visual art, with a vision that extends to music, dance, and other creative disciplines over time.

**Who it serves:** Artists who are blind or low vision, community members, donors, arts patrons, educators, cultural partners.

**Tone:** Expressive · Celebratory · Community-rooted

**Lead message:** Creative expression belongs to everyone. The Envision Arts Center is where that belief becomes a place.

**What Thriving looks like here:** An artist whose work is seen, valued, and celebrated. A community built around creative possibility.

**Content rule:** Never frame arts programming as therapy or rehabilitation. These are artists. Lead with the work and the space, not the diagnosis. Language should be expansive enough to grow with the program.

## Envision Base Supply Center

**What it does:** Retail locations on military installations serving active duty service members, veterans, and military families. Military-facing merchandise and BSC-specific e-commerce. Operates under AbilityOne contracts — AbilityOne is the contract foundation here, not just a credential.

**Who it serves:** Active duty service members, veterans, military families, installation leadership, contracting officers.

**Tone:** Direct · Earned · No-nonsense

**Lead message:** Quality products, reliable supply, operated by people who understand service. The workforce behind the counter has something in common with the people in front of it.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A service member who gets what they need without friction. A veteran employee who found purpose and income after service.

**Content rule:** Avoid "giving back" or "supporting our troops" framing — it reads as performative to this audience. Lead with operational reliability and shared values. US-based workforce is a strong proof point. **Envision Xpress is retired** — all materials must reflect Envision Base Supply Center going forward.

## Envision Child Development Center

**What it does:** Early childhood education at two campuses — Wichita and Dallas (1801 Valley View Lane, Farmers Branch, TX). Primary focus is children who are blind or low vision, in an integrated classroom environment where sighted peers learn alongside them.

**Who it serves:** Parents and caregivers of young children, particularly families navigating early childhood vision loss. Also serves the broader Wichita and Farmers Branch communities through an integrated classroom model.

**Tone:** Warm · Community-driven · Parent-facing

**Lead message:** Every child is ready to learn. ECDC builds the environment that proves it — state-of-the-art facilities, future-ready curriculum, and deep community roots where kids who are blind or low vision have every opportunity to thrive alongside their peers.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A child who enters kindergarten ready — socially, emotionally, academically. A family that found a community that truly understood what their child needed from the start.

**Content rule:** The integration model is the differentiator — lead with that. Use "future-ready" not "forward-thinking." **Do not use "all abilities"** — overstates scope. **Do not use "difference"** in reference to students. Community involvement is a proof point.

## Envision Commercial Solutions

**What it does:** Print services, contact center, fulfillment, assembly, products, and e-commerce (Supply Source). Envision's primary commercial revenue engine. Serves enterprise and government buyers across any industry.

- **Track 1 — Commercial/Enterprise:** Private sector buyers across any industry. Procurement managers, supply chain leaders, operations and logistics teams, corporate clients. AbilityOne is not relevant here. Capability and certification lead everything.
- **Track 2 — Government:** General government contracts where compliance and certification matter. AbilityOne is important but not always the primary contract vehicle. BSC contracts are addressed in the Base Supply Center pillar.

**Who it serves:** Procurement officers, supply chain managers, contracting officers, GPO decision-makers, enterprise buyers across industries.

**Tone:** Business-first · Capability-led · Credibility-forward

**Lead message:** We are a high-performing commercial partner. Capability, compliance, and on-time delivery are the story. Mission is the reason to choose us when capability is equal.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A workforce producing enterprise-grade output. Clients who get what they need, on time, at spec.

**Content rule:** Lead always with capability, compliance, and proof points. **Mission enters only when the contract requires it or the buyer is known to value nonprofit partnerships.** Never open with nonprofit or charity framing. The mission-follows-credibility rule applies here more than anywhere else.

## Envision Mission Services

**What it does:** Community-based programs and services supporting BVI individuals across every stage of life — from early childhood through senior years. Programs span recreation, socialization, daily living support, and community connection, including but not limited to Heather's Camp, PRIDE (Adult Day Support), and Adult Support Group.

**Who it serves:** BVI individuals of all ages and their families, caregivers, social workers, and community partners.

**Tone:** Compassionate · Community-centered · Dignified

**Lead message:** From the earliest years to the latest ones, Envision Mission Services is there. Whatever the stage, whatever the need, no one navigates this alone.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A person who has community, connection, and support that fits their life at every stage. A family that isn't figuring this out alone.

**Content rule:** Person-first language is non-negotiable here. No inspiration porn. Stories should center the person's experience and choices — not Envision's role in them. **Never enumerate all programs** — the scope is too broad. Lead with the continuum, not a checklist.

## Envision Research Institute

**What it does:** Research labs and facilities, fellowship programs, and collaborative projects with universities and industry partners. Focused on vision science and innovations that improve quality of life for people who are blind or low vision.

**Who it serves:** Researchers, fellows, university and industry partners, healthcare and policy audiences, donors motivated by scientific impact.

**Tone:** Credible · Precise · Forward-looking

**Lead message:** The future of vision health is being built here. Envision Research Institute turns scientific discovery into real tools, real treatments, and real change for people who are blind or low vision.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A research breakthrough that changes how vision loss is understood or treated. A fellow who goes on to lead the field. A partnership that produces something that didn't exist before.

**Content rule:** **Specificity is credibility here.** Name the partnerships, the studies, the outcomes where possible. Vague innovation language undermines trust with this audience more than anywhere else in the brand.

## Envision Vision Rehabilitation Center

**What it does:** A comprehensive vision rehabilitation center offering clinical low vision evaluations, assistive technology training, and independent living skills programs. Also home to the Everyday Store, which provides adaptive products and tools for daily living.

**Who it serves:** People who are blind or low vision — newly diagnosed or experiencing progressive vision loss — referring physicians, caregivers, family members.

**Tone:** Warm · Clinically credible · Hope-forward

**Lead message:** You don't have to figure this out alone. There are clinical experts, assistive technology specialists, and independence training programs here, all designed around what you actually need to live fully.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A person who moves through the world with confidence, equipped with the right tools and skills. Not overcoming vision loss — building a full life alongside it.

**Content rule:** **Never use "despite"** in relation to vision loss. Never frame services as charity. Center the client's agency. Referring physicians are a key referral source — materials targeting them lead with clinical outcomes and program credibility, not emotional storytelling.

## Envision Workforce Innovation

**What it does:** Career development, job readiness, and employment pathways for people who are blind or low vision. Youth programs include Level-Up — a national residential Pre-Employment Transition Services program for ages 14-22 held at Wichita State University. Adult programs include Pathways and the Workforce Readiness Bootcamp.

**Who it serves:** Youth ages 14-22 who are blind or low vision and their TVIs, parents, and VR counselors. Adults who are blind or low vision seeking employment or advancement. Employers, workforce development partners, and vocational rehabilitation referral sources.

**Tone:** Motivating · Practical · Outcome-focused

**Lead message:** A career isn't just income. It's identity, independence, and momentum. Envision Workforce Innovation builds the path and walks it with you, at every age and every stage.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A young person with direction and the skills to pursue it. An adult who lands a job and keeps it. Employers who come back because the candidates are prepared.

**Content rule:** Lead with outcomes and proof points, not inspiration. **For Level-Up, the primary marketing audience is TVIs and VR counselors** — lead with program structure and Pre-ETS compliance. For adult programs, lead with real skills and real outcomes.

## Continuing Education

**What it does:** Grand Rounds, Envision Conference, and on-demand courses. Continuing education for professionals in the vision rehabilitation and blindness services fields. Known internally as Envision University. Externally referenced as Continuing Education.

**Who it serves:** Vision rehabilitation specialists, occupational therapists, orientation and mobility specialists, low vision clinicians, researchers, allied health professionals.

**Tone:** Authoritative · Collegial · Peer-to-peer

**Lead message:** The field moves forward when practitioners do. Envision brings together the best minds in vision rehabilitation to share what's working, what's new, and what's next.

**What Thriving looks like here:** A clinician who leaves with tools they use Monday morning. A field that is more connected, more informed, and better equipped.

**Content rule:** This audience is highly credentialed — write to their level. Lead with content quality, faculty, and networking opportunity. CEU credits are a practical proof point. **No standalone logo** — appears as Envision master logo with program name as text treatment only.
