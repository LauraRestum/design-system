# Brand Rules

The non-negotiable rules that govern all Envision work. If a rule here conflicts with any other document, this file wins. Sourced from the Envision Brand Book (2026 Edition).

---

## The Through-Line

Everything Envision does connects to one idea: **Thriving.**

Not surviving. Not managing. Thriving — with the right tools, the right support, and the right opportunities at every stage of life.

## The Mission and Credibility Rule

> **Mission follows credibility.**

Establish that Envision can do the work. Then mission becomes the reason to choose Envision over an equally capable partner.

In commercial contexts, lead with capability, compliance, and proof points. Mission enters only when the contract requires it or the buyer is known to value nonprofit partnerships. **Never open with nonprofit or charity framing in a commercial sales context.**

In mission-driven contexts, lead with the person's experience and agency. Envision's role is enabling — not rescuing.

## Language Rules

### Person-first language is non-negotiable

- On first mention: **"people who are blind or low vision"** (BVI is acceptable after that).
- Never "the blind," "the visually impaired," or "the disabled."

### No deficit language

Never use: *suffers, victim, afflicted, wheelchair-bound, despite vision loss.*

### Specific swaps

| Instead of | Use |
| --- | --- |
| "suffers from vision loss" | "lives with vision loss" |
| "the blind" | "people who are blind or low vision" |
| "normal vision" | "sighted" or "typical vision" |
| "seeing eye dog" | "guide dog" |
| "despite vision loss" | "with the right tools and support" |
| "assist" | "help," "support," or "partner" |
| "provide accommodations" | "design with access from the start" |
| "return to a normal life" | "return to daily routines / independence" |
| "optimize" | "improve" |
| "initiate" | "start" |

### Agency sits with the person

People thrive. Organizations and tools enable. Never "we make people thrive" — that's paternalistic.

### No sight-based idioms in BVI-facing content

Prefer "experience," "understand," and "find" over "see," "look," and "watch."

### No em dashes

Brand voice rule. Use commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead.

### No AI-sounding phrasing

Avoid stock AI cadences: "Let's dive in," "It's important to note," "In today's fast-paced world," etc.

### Case

Sentence case always — except where brand standards specifically require otherwise (buttons, large subheads).

### Donor names

Used only in donor-facing contexts. Drop donor names in all general materials.

### Measurable claims

Keep claims measurable where possible. Stats, outcomes, proof points over vague "impact" language.

## Colors

- Colors come from `tokens/colors.json`. Do not invent new colors.
- **Primary Blue `#003087`** (PMS 287C) — the default lead color.
- **Primary Green `#78BE21`** (PMS 368C) — controlled accent only. Never a field color.
- **Body copy** on light backgrounds: Charcoal `#53565A`. **Never pure black.**
- **Headlines** on light backgrounds: Primary Blue `#003087` or Navy `#002855`.
- Text on color backgrounds must use one of the eight WCAG AA-approved combinations listed in `tokens/colors.json` under `accessibility.approved-combinations`.

## Typography

- **Gotham** — print and designed materials (requires license).
- **Montserrat** — web, HTML, and digital only.
- **Humanist** — reserved exclusively for the logo. Never used elsewhere.
- Headlines: Bold or Black, title case, line-height 110%, letter-spacing -0.045em.
- Body copy: Regular/Book, line-height 140-160%, letter-spacing 0 to -0.02em.
- Buttons: Bold uppercase, letter-spacing 0.25em.
- Large subheads: Bold uppercase, letter-spacing 0.5em.

## Geometry

- `border-radius` max 4px. No rounded pills.
- No `box-shadow` in production output.
- No gradient backgrounds except on approved callout/chart patterns.

## Logo

- Always use the horizontal master logo unless constraints require a variation.
- Clear space: at least the height of the "E" in Envision on all sides.
- **Never:** rotate, stretch, recolor, add effects/shadows/outlines, place on distracting backgrounds, alter spacing or proportions, rearrange components, or recreate with a different typeface.
- See `guidelines/brand-architecture.md` for sub-brand lock-up rules.

## Imagery

- Candid, natural photography that reflects the diversity and vibrancy of the people Envision serves.
- Avoid staged or generic stock photography.
- Highlight moments of connection, achievement, and community.
- Use creative depth of field to draw attention to the subject.
- **Never** use imagery that frames people with vision loss as passive, pitied, or defined by their disability.
